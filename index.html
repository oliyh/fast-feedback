How do we improve things?

  1. Understand
  2. Observe
  3. Think
  4. Change
  5. GOTO 1

We may loop hundreds of times to fix a bug or implement a feature, so it's important to optimise it

Our job is to improve things. We may be adding features, fixing bugs or making things more efficient, but usally the overall flow is the same

  1. Understand: gather information - description, wireframes, plan
  2. Observe: reproduce, or attempt to perform
  3. Think: documentation, pair
  4. Change: Typing time - concise code, well-factored code

Understand, observe, think

  • Build a mental model
  • Take notes
    • Mind maps
    • Lists
    • Kettelkasten method
  • Challenge, update and reinforce your model

A honed mental model will help you to understand and think more efficiently about problems and solutions.

  • Absorb any information you already have to start building your model. It should make intuitive sense
  • Take notes but just as important is to know how to organise them
  • Never be afraid to challenge and update your model - it can only make it better. Ask questions and experiment

Organise!

Accept you will be initially deluged with information

  • It might all be relevant: some for now, the rest in the future
  • It won't all fit in your head, so take notes
  • Arrange your knowledge methodically to allow you to focus on the things that matter without being overwhelmed

Changing

Our IDEs are anvils

keyboards our hammers

Once we've done all our thinking we are ready to write our new code.

IDE

Your IDE will contain many shortcuts for common tasks:

  • Auto-formatting
  • Renaming something
  • Auto imports of dependencies
  • There will be many, many more
  • Spend some time discovering and playing with these. Having them at your fingertips will multiply your output

Typing

Typing speed ∝ Hacking ability

  • If you can type 150 words per minute you can hack any system, no matter how secure

Keyboards

  • Do you use all your fingers?
  • Touch type without looking at the keyboard?
  • Have frequently used keys in convenient places?
  • Minimise finger travel to improve accuracy?

  • Can you think faster than you type? If so, your typing speed is holding you back
  • I thought I was good at typing until I got a new keyboard
  • It forced me to learn to type from scratch, and I used the opportunity to optimise it

Interactive

Interactive development separates state from code

  • Allows hot reloading without losing context
  • Feedback loop takes less than a second

Result

Fast feedback means we can have greater appetite for exploring edge cases

A QA engineer walks into a bar. Orders a beer. Orders 0 beers. Orders 999999999999 beers. Orders a lizard. Orders -1 beers. Orders 0.1 beers. Orders a uieibaclkjd.
  • Instead of being weary after a long effort just to get it working, you can be fresh and eager to make it better
  • Make it work, make it right, make it fast
